Insights on ARM’s Future in HPC/AI

ARM is projected to improve vector capabilities (SVE2), integrate HBM, and enhance interconnects by 2028, making it competitive for HPC workloads. By 2030, ARM could reach 60 TFLOPS per socket with 0.95 Perf/Watt, challenging x86 in many areas.

Competitive Analysis

Intel Xeon remains dominant for HPC and AI training due to ecosystem maturity and memory bandwidth. AMD EPYC competes strongly in core density and efficiency. ARM leads in energy efficiency and cloud-native workloads, while GPUs dominate AI training performance.
